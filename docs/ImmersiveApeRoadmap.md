# Immersive Ape Roadmap

Immersive Ape is being structured as a 100-cycle climb toward a playable demo, with each cycle intended to land a shippable increment rather than a speculative rewrite.

## Cycle Groups

- Cycles 1-10: Boot the full-screen Metal shell, connect it to ApeSDK state, establish the follow-camera, build the first terrain/ocean/weather rendering path, and make ape selection stable.
- Cycles 11-20: Replace placeholder meshes with robust procedural world generation driven by land genetics, location, tide, and weather fields. Land the first biome palette and stronger day/night transitions.
- Cycles 21-30: Expand flora generation into distinct coastal, grassland, bushland, and forest layers with DNA-aware variation and higher polygon fidelity.
- Cycles 31-40: Add richer ape body generation, locomotion, silhouettes, gait variation, and animation states for wandering, pausing, social proximity, and speaking.
- Cycles 41-50: Surface food seeking and foraging clearly in-world by rendering food classes, local abundance, search behavior, and outcome feedback around the selected ape.
- Cycles 51-60: Translate ApeSDK social systems into spatial storytelling with meeting behaviors, speech effects, memory traces, territory cues, and better visibility of friendships and conflicts.
- Cycles 61-70: Deepen sky, cloud, lighting, rain, fog, and surf rendering so that weather and time-of-day become major visual systems instead of simple overlays.
- Cycles 71-80: Improve interaction flow by introducing a proper ape-selection layer, lightweight camera control options, accessibility support, and the first floating-window mode.
- Cycles 81-90: Add audio, ambience, performance budgeting, LOD tuning, and stability work needed for longer sessions on Mac hardware.
- Cycles 91-100: Focus on demo readiness with polish, balance, onboarding, regression coverage, packaging, and a vertically complete “playable demo” pass.

## Current State

- Cycle 1 is the vertical slice foundation: a programmatic full-screen Metal app path, a renderer-facing ApeSDK snapshot bridge, a follow-the-selected-ape camera, and the first procedural environment pass.
- Cycle 2 deepens the readable demo slice: the immersive HUD now surfaces ape drives, goals, and state, while the renderer adds stronger rain, starfield, surf, and process-marking cues around food and nearby apes.
- Cycle 3 turns nearby ape activity into clearer spatial storytelling: the Mac HUD now carries encounter summaries and scene narration, the renderer draws social-contact ribbons for conversation/conflict/grooming/courtship, and the follow camera biases itself to keep important encounters readable.
- Cycle 4 moves the viewpoint closer to the selected ape itself: the immersive camera now uses state-aware embodied posture, and the renderer draws a foreground forelimb/chest layer so travel, feeding, swimming, conflict, and rest feel more like inhabiting the ape than observing it.
- Cycle 5 makes the selected ape's intent explicit: the renderer now picks a current attention target from food, nearby apes, courtship, conflict, or rest, draws a guide path and target marker into the world, and biases the follow camera and HUD toward that active focus.
- Cycle 6 softens the procedural world itself: terrain, coastline, beach, surf-edge, and cloud masking now interpolate between sampled points so landforms and weather fields read as curved transitions rather than blocky square steps.
- Cycle 7 lifts the interpolated weather field into the sky: overhead cloud masses now derive from the same smoothed cloud samples as the terrain patch, so the sky reads as a coherent rounded system rather than a separate set of random billows.
- Cycle 8 turns the interpolated coast into a fuller shoreline system: terrain, shallow water, and a dedicated surf-break layer now share the same shoreline sampling, which darkens wet sand and lays rolling foam over the rounded beach edge instead of leaving the coast as a simple land-water boundary.
- Cycle 9 pulls time-of-day back down onto the sea: the renderer now adds a sun-or-moon reflection path over deeper water and darkens water and surf more convincingly under cloud cover, so the coast responds to the sky instead of only sitting beneath it.
- Cycle 10 lets the sky travel across the land: the same interpolated cloud field now drives moving daylight shadow across terrain and vegetation, with low-sun warmth preserved between shadow bands so the world reads as weather passing over a single connected scene.
- Cycle 11 starts the biome palette layer promised for the next roadmap band: coastal sand, meadow, scrub, forest, and stone now use more intentional terrain and vegetation palettes, so the procedural world reads as differentiated regions instead of a mostly uniform green-brown surface.
- Cycle 12 deepens that biome layer into form: the same coastal, meadow, scrub, forest, and stone regions now push different vegetation silhouettes and rock clustering, so the world separates by shape instead of only by color.
- Cycle 13 turns those biome forms into habitat patches: vegetation now follows low-frequency density bands, creating dune clusters, meadow flower pockets, scrub tufts, forest floor litter, and stony clearings so the procedural world reads as regions with internal structure rather than evenly scattered props.
- Cycle 14 softens the seams between those biome patches: terrain now picks up ecotone tinting near neighboring materials, and vegetation adds edge-specific growth so coast-to-meadow margins, meadow-to-forest edges, scrub bands, and rocky fringes read as transitions instead of hard switches.
- Cycle 15 turns time-of-day into a fuller atmospheric system: dawn, dusk, and night now drive horizon glow, celestial halo, richer fog tinting, and low-light water color shifts so the world feels lit by changing air as well as changing sky.
- Cycle 16 teaches the terrain to read as relief: the renderer now measures local slope, ridges, basins, and runoff so exposed high ground picks up warmer stony accents, low sheltered ground cools toward damp green, and vegetation density follows landform instead of only biome color.
- Cycle 17 turns that relief into visible geology: runoff bands now lay damp traces and pebble deposits through low basins while steeper exposed ground sheds small scree clusters, so the terrain reads as shaped by drainage and collapse rather than only painted by elevation.
- Cycle 18 lets weather settle onto that landform system: rain now darkens inland ground, fills sheltered basins with reflective puddle pools, and makes runoff corridors respond to current weather instead of reading as permanently dry marks.
- Cycle 19 gives that inland water a living surface: runoff traces now carry animated sheen drifting downslope while rain pools layer moving ripple bands, so recent weather reads as active motion instead of static wet decals.
- Cycle 20 gives that water a terrain fringe: runoff now fans fine sediment into low ground while puddles form muddy rims and wet sedge tufts, so rain reads as reshaping surface edges rather than only darkening or animating them.
- Cycle 21 lets that wet ground breathe into the air: strong runoff corridors and pooled basins now lift low drifting haze, especially in twilight or fresh rain, so the aftermath reads as humid atmosphere instead of only altered terrain.
- Cycle 22 lets the flora inherit clearer genetic identity: the renderer now derives a biome DNA profile from the selected ape and world seed, pushes canopy, understory, bloom, drift, and rock clustering with it, and layers extra meadow, scrub, forest, coast, and stone details so vegetation reads as lineage-shaped habitat instead of a generic prop pass.
- Cycle 23 turns that DNA into clearer habitat signatures: coastlines now lift reed clusters, meadows grow seedhead stands, scrub picks up fruiting thickets, forests gain fern fans, and stony ground carries lichen-marked clusters, so each biome reads as a stacked flora layer instead of the same prop family recolored by terrain.
- Cycle 24 lets those signatures gather into microhabitats: reed beds now thicken through wet pockets, meadows collect seed-rich swales, scrub chains into thorny copses on dry ridges, forests mass ferny hollows under shelter, and stone ground stacks lichen gardens with talus tufts, so each biome reads as local colonies instead of evenly spaced specimen props.
- Cycle 25 brings the new flora stack up to closer-view fidelity: signature plants now sprout secondary leaves, stems, berry clusters, inner fronds, and broken talus companions, so reed beds, meadow swales, scrub copses, fern hollows, and lichen gardens read as sculpted habitat meshes instead of sparse primitive markers.
- Cycle 26 lets that flora carry visible weather posture: coastal reeds, meadow grasses, scrub, flowers, and forest ferns now derive bend, droop, and spread from wind, rain, relief, shelter, and ape DNA, so the biome layers read as weather-shaped growth instead of every soft plant standing in the same neutral pose.
- Cycle 27 mixes juvenile and mature growth into the same biome patches: coasts now sprout fresh shoots beside older reeds, meadows mix low regrowth with heavy heads, scrub alternates woody cores and softer pups, forests layer fern pups with sapling undergrowth, and stone ground picks up sparse pioneer growth, so the habitat colonies read as living succession instead of one repeated growth stage.
- Cycle 28 lets those flora colonies spread directionally: wind and landform now pull coastal shoots, meadow regrowth, scrub offshoots, fern spill, and rocky pioneer growth into short dispersal trails beyond their core patches, so each habitat reads as a reproducing system instead of a sealed clump of props.
- Cycle 29 gives that flora stack visible phenology: light, rain, moisture, and ape DNA now push fresh shoots, blooming meadows, berry-heavy scrub, fern flush, and dry or reviving stone pioneers through distinct phase accents, so the habitat layers read as living growth states instead of static arrangements frozen at one moment.
- Cycle 30 completes that flora band with habitat hierarchy: each biome now grows occasional landmark specimens, with tall reed sentinels on coasts, flowering meadow crowns, knotted scrub anchors, canopy-backed forest groves, and stacked lichen outcrops on stone, so the vegetation reads as a full scale range instead of only patch-detail repeated across the terrain.
- Cycle 31 begins ape embodiment with individualized body profiles: nearby apes and the selected first-person body now derive torso, hip, shoulder, muzzle, limb, and crest proportions from each being's height, mass, frame, age, sex, pregnancy, hair, and current state, so the population reads as distinct bodies instead of one shared mannequin.
- Cycle 32 gives those bodies a readable motion language: nearby apes and the selected first-person embodiment now derive wandering stride, pause-settle breathing, social lean, and speaking gestures from state and encounter context, so locomotion and close interaction read as distinct animation beats instead of one shared swing loop.
- Cycle 33 gives each ape a distinct gait signature: cadence, lead side, torso sway, shoulder and hip roll, foot lift, and head bob now derive from body traits, energy, and honor, so nearby travel and the embodied forelimb layer read as individuals with different locomotion rhythms instead of synchronized copies of the same stride.
- Cycle 34 sharpens ape silhouettes into readable stance states: wandering bodies now lean and open into travel, paused apes settle and narrow, social proximity pulls posture inward, and speaking lifts chest, head, crest, and hands, so behavior reads at a glance even before the motion loop or speech markers are obvious.
- Cycle 35 moves ape avatars onto a shared skeleton: nearby ape bodies now derive spine, shoulder, hip, elbow, wrist, knee, and ankle joints from height, age, and DNA-facing traits, and the selected embodiment inherits the same skeletal scaling cues, so ape bodies can change coherently instead of only stretching isolated meshes.
- Cycle 36 carries that skeleton out to the extremities: hands and feet now derive palms, finger reach, thumb spread, soles, heels, and toe pads from height, age, and DNA-facing traits, so ape avatars read as full bodies instead of a detailed torso with generic limb-end spheres.
- Cycle 37 gives nearby apes readable faces: eyes, pupils, ears, nostrils, and lower-jaw posture now derive from DNA-facing traits and current behavior, while social, feeding, and conflict states steer gaze so attention reads from the head instead of only speech bubbles or body stance.
- Cycle 38 lets nearby apes aim that face as a full head pose: ear tilt, brow posture, cheek emphasis, jaw line, and eye placement now ride state-aware head yaw and pitch from food, social focus, conflict, and rest, so attention reads from the whole head silhouette instead of only moving pupils.
- Cycle 39 gives nearby apes personal space inside the immersive scene: nearby positions now run through a local clearance solver that keeps apes separated from the selected body, from one another, from food props, and away from waterlogged or clutter-heavy terrain pockets, so groupings stay readable without bodies or scene elements intersecting.
- Cycle 40 plants nearby apes into the landscape: each nearby body now derives a local up/right/forward basis from terrain or shoreline normals and carries it through the shared skeleton, feet, face, and state effects, so slopes and coastal edges read as grounded stance and locomotion instead of upright mannequins pasted onto uneven ground.
- Cycle 41 begins the foraging band with abundance readback: food props now project type-specific transparent field cues scaled by intensity, and the attention/HUD food focus names those targets as rich patches, steady patches, or light finds, so sparse picks and high-yield foraging spots read before the selected ape closes the distance.
- Cycle 42 gives food focus its own search behavior: foraging attention now layers a low scan cone and moving type-specific search traces between the selected ape and the current food target, so hunger reads as active seeking and sampling rather than the same generic guide used for social or travel focus.
- Cycle 43 turns food attention into feeding payoff: once the selected ape is eating, the search-only forage cues yield to type-specific intake effects around the embodied hands and chest, and the focus/HUD language shifts from tracking to feeding, so foraging reads as a completed outcome instead of a search line that never resolves.
- Cycle 44 gives failed foraging its own scarcity readback: when the selected ape comes up empty, the forage focus shifts into depleted target cues and muted empty-hand sampling effects around the embodied chest and hands, while the focus/HUD language names scarce or absent finds directly, so hunger setbacks read as visible outcomes instead of reverting to the normal search line.
- Cycle 45 makes food choice visible: active forage focus now keeps a secondary nearby food option in play, drawing a dim comparison branch to that alternate patch and naming what the selected ape is favoring over competing food classes or yields, so hunger reads as weighing options instead of tunneling toward one arbitrary target.
- Cycle 46 gives active foraging an embodied handling posture: while the selected ape is still tracking food, the first-person body and camera now settle into food-class-aware pluck, strip, pry, cradle, or gather poses based on the current target, and the focus copy names that handling mode directly, so hunger reads through the body before feeding payoff begins.
- Cycle 47 gives foraging a visible approach phase: once the selected ape closes on a target but before feeding starts, the wide search field now collapses into a local preparation zone around the food while the first-person hands and focus copy shift into imminent take-up cues, so foraging reads as an approach and readying beat instead of jumping straight from tracking to eating.
- Cycle 48 lets the food source itself react: the active target patch now dims and collapses as attention commits to it, then shows type-specific harvest or depleted remnants during feeding and failed foraging states, so hunger outcomes read at the food site as well as in the selected body's hands and chest.
- Cycle 49 grounds food choice in the landscape: when the selected ape is weighing two nearby options, the preferred patch now reads as the pulled-forward site while the alternate patch stays visibly deferred, so choice remains legible even when attention is on the food sources instead of the guide line.
- Cycle 50 completes the foraging band with neighborhood context: the immersive view now summarizes whether food is pooling ahead, scattered to the flanks, or thinning out locally, and renders that same directional context around the selected ape so the whole food story reads from local landscape pressure down to the chosen patch and final outcome.
- Cycle 51 begins the social band with meeting behavior: active social focus now resolves into mode-specific approach and arrival zones for conversation, grooming, courtship, companionship, and conflict, while the selected embodiment and focus language adopt matching meeting posture, so ape-to-ape encounters read as distinct social events instead of one generic target line.
- Cycle 52 gives those social encounters a speech field: conversation and voiced social states now expand into directional plumes, pulse lanes, and embodied throat/chest resonance around the active encounter, while the focus and story text acknowledge calls, chatter, and threat vocalization directly, so communication reads as active voice instead of only proximity and posture.
- Cycle 53 gives social attention memory traces: nearby apes now carry selected-relative familiarity, attraction, relationship, and friend-or-foe history into the immersive bridge, and the renderer turns that into trailing recall strands, embodied remembrance cues, and HUD language for kin, trusted, tense, drawn, or merely familiar encounters, so social focus reads as history-bearing memory instead of only the current meeting.
- Cycle 54 gives those social encounters territory pressure: nearby ape snapshots now carry both the other ape's own familiarity and the selected ape's familiarity at that encounter site, while the renderer turns that into home-ground, shared-ground, border, and crossing cues in the world, the selected body, and the HUD/story text, so social focus reads as happening on known or contested ground instead of in abstract empty space.
- Cycle 55 makes friendships and conflicts legible as social pull: nearby ape snapshots now classify kinship, trust, attraction, wariness, and rivalry into a dedicated tie layer, and the renderer turns those ties into guide, body, and encounter cues so who the selected ape is drawn toward or bracing against reads before the meeting fully resolves.
- Cycle 56 makes social rank readable inside those ties: selected-relative honor now classifies encounters into higher-rank pressure, peer standing, or the selected ape holding rank, and the renderer turns that into guide slope, embodied posture, and HUD/story language so status reads before companionship, grooming, or conflict fully settles.
- Cycle 57 makes episodic recall readable inside those encounters: nearby ape snapshots now carry selected-relative episodic event, affect, recency, and whether the memory is firsthand or heard as anecdote or intention, and the renderer turns that into recall fields, body cues, and HUD/story language so remembered episodes and retold stories read before the present encounter fully resolves.
- Cycle 58 makes caregiving readable inside those encounters: nearby ape snapshots now carry whether an ape is actively carrying the young, and the renderer combines that with suckling state and close parent-child ties into a dedicated caregiving mode, with cradle cues in the guide, body, and encounter field plus HUD/story language so care reads distinctly from generic companionship.
- Future cycles should preserve the Swift-first app structure and only drop into C when the simulation core or low-level bridge genuinely benefits from it.
